About Carbon Footprint Calculator

Learn about how we calculate your carbon footprint and the methodology behind our calculations.

What is a Carbon Footprint?

A carbon footprint is the total amount of greenhouse gases (including carbon dioxide and methane) that are generated by our actions. The average carbon footprint for a person in India is approximately 1.9 tons per year, which is significantly lower than the global average but is growing rapidly with economic development.

Globally, the average carbon footprint is closer to 4 tons. To have the best chance of avoiding a 2℃ rise in global temperatures, the average global carbon footprint per person needs to drop to under 2 tons by 2050. As India continues to develop, balancing economic growth with sustainable practices becomes increasingly important.

Our Calculation Methodology

Our calculator estimates your carbon footprint based on your inputs in three main categories:

Transportation

Car Travel

We calculate emissions based on kilometers driven and your vehicle's fuel efficiency (km/liter). The formula uses the standard emission factor of 2.31 kg CO₂e per liter of petrol.

Car emissions = (Kilometers driven × 52 weeks) ÷ km/liter × 2.31 kg CO₂e/liter
Two-Wheeler Travel

Emissions from motorcycles and scooters (very common in India) use the same factor of 2.31 kg CO₂e per liter but generally higher efficiency.

Two-wheeler emissions = (Kilometers driven × 52 weeks) ÷ km/liter × 2.31 kg CO₂e/liter
Auto Rickshaw

Emissions from auto rickshaws (common in Indian cities) use an average factor of 0.08 kg CO₂e per passenger kilometer.

Auto rickshaw emissions = Kilometers traveled × 52 weeks × 0.08 kg CO₂e/km
Public Transit

Emissions from public transit like buses and metro use an average factor of 0.04 kg CO₂e per passenger kilometer in India.

Public transit emissions = Kilometers traveled × 52 weeks × 0.04 kg CO₂e/km
Air Travel

For simplicity in the Indian context, we categorize flights as domestic or international:

  • Domestic flights (within India): 300 kg CO₂e per round trip
  • International flights (from India to other countries): 2000 kg CO₂e per round trip

Home Energy

Electricity

Emissions from electricity use the U.S. average emission factor of 0.45 kg CO₂e per kWh. This varies by region based on the energy mix (coal, natural gas, nuclear, renewables).

Electricity emissions = kWh per month × 12 months × 0.45 kg CO₂e/kWh
Natural Gas

Natural gas emissions are calculated using the standard factor of 5.3 kg CO₂e per therm.

Natural gas emissions = Therms per month × 12 months × 5.3 kg CO₂e/therm
Heating Oil

Heating oil emissions are calculated using the factor of 10.16 kg CO₂e per gallon.

Heating oil emissions = Gallons per month × 12 months × 10.16 kg CO₂e/gallon

Food

Food emissions are estimated based on diet type using these annual averages:

  • Omnivore (regular meat consumption): 1,500 kg CO₂e/year
  • Flexitarian (occasional meat): 1,100 kg CO₂e/year
  • Pescatarian (fish, no meat): 900 kg CO₂e/year
  • Vegetarian (no meat or fish): 700 kg CO₂e/year
  • Vegan (no animal products): 500 kg CO₂e/year

Data Sources

Our emission factors are derived from multiple reputable sources, including:

  • U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA)
  • Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC)
  • U.S. Energy Information Administration (EIA)
  • International Energy Agency (IEA)

Limitations

While our calculator provides a reasonable estimate of your carbon footprint, it has limitations:

  • Regional variations in emission factors are not fully accounted for
  • Simplified assumptions about lifestyle patterns
  • Does not account for all sources of emissions (e.g., goods purchased)
  • Based on average emission factors that may not reflect your specific situation
