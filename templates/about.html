About the Carbon Footprint Calculator

Methodology

This calculator provides an estimate of your carbon footprint based on your lifestyle choices and consumption patterns. The carbon footprint is measured in kilograms of carbon dioxide equivalent (kg CO₂e), which includes the impact of various greenhouse gases converted to the equivalent amount of CO₂.

The calculations are based on average emission factors from reputable sources, including the EPA (Environmental Protection Agency), the Department of Energy, and peer-reviewed scientific research.

Calculation Factors

Transportation
  • Car emissions: Based on fuel efficiency (MPG) and distance traveled
  • Public transportation: Average emissions per passenger mile
  • Flights: Categorized by distance
    • Short flights (<1000 miles): ~500 kg CO₂e per flight
    • Medium flights (1000-3000 miles): ~1200 kg CO₂e per flight
    • Long flights (>3000 miles): ~3000 kg CO₂e per flight
Home Energy
  • Electricity: ~0.45 kg CO₂e per kWh (US average)
  • Natural gas: ~5.3 kg CO₂e per therm
  • Heating oil: ~10.15 kg CO₂e per gallon
Food & Consumption
  • Vegan diet: ~1500 kg CO₂e per year
  • Vegetarian diet: ~2000 kg CO₂e per year
  • Pescatarian diet: ~2500 kg CO₂e per year
  • Omnivore diet: ~3000 kg CO₂e per year
  • High meat diet: ~4000 kg CO₂e per year

Limitations

While this calculator provides a good estimate of your carbon footprint, it has some limitations:

  • The calculator uses average emission factors that may not perfectly match your specific situation
  • Some emissions sources might not be captured (e.g., specific consumer goods)
  • Regional variations in electricity grid emissions are not accounted for
  • The calculator provides annual estimates rather than real-time tracking

Why Reduce Your Carbon Footprint?

Climate change is one of the most pressing challenges facing our planet today. By reducing your carbon footprint, you can:

  • Help combat global climate change
  • Reduce air and water pollution
  • Conserve natural resources for future generations
  • Often save money through reduced energy consumption
  • Inspire others to make positive changes

Every action, no matter how small, contributes to the collective effort to create a more sustainable future.
